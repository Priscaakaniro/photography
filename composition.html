Composition in photography

Composition in simple terms, means the constituents of your photo.
It is basically what made up the shot you took.
The items in the picture, people, the arrangement of the elements in the picture, all pieced together, make up the composition of the picture.
Your composition strongly determines the quality of your picture. When you have a very good composition, it certainly leads to a great picture and the reverse is the case.

When deciding the composition of your picture, keep in mind that simple setups thrive more than complicated set ups. An architect designer called Ludwig Mies van der Rohe is often associated with the saying “less is more”from Andrea del Sarto, 1855, a poem by Robert Browning which basically suggests that the simpler, the better.
It is a great quote to live buy especially in art related things and areas, and your photography is your art.

Position and angles

The position and angles are two elements that has great impact on the outcome of your shots.
There are 3 main types of position and angles, which we would discuss in a bit.
First, what is the definition of position and angles in relation to photography?

Position in simple terms refer to the height the camera is held from in relation to the ground. The position you choose to shoot your subject from greatly influences the outcome.

There are 3 basic positions to keep in mind when making your composition, they are:
Eye-level position: this means holding the camera at a position normal to your eye.

High position: this means holding the camera at a position higher than your eye-level. You can achieve this position by raising your arms or with the aid of a higher platform, like a foot stool or crate.

Low position: this means holding the camera at a position lower than your eye-level. This position can be achieved by squatting or lying down or simply bringing your arms lower than your eye-level.

Angles are basically the degree from which the camera is pointing to the subject. As much as having a clear subject would help you achieve a clear and good shot, shooting from the correct angle and position would help enhance the quality of the shot.

There are 3 basic angles to keep in mind when making your composition, they are:
Eye-level angle: this means holding the camera at a horizontal level.

High angle: this means holding the camera in a way that makes the camera face downwards.

Low angle: this means holding the camera in a way that makes the camera face upwards.
